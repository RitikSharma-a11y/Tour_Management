MacChief
Oklahoma City, Oklahoma 
  City of Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada
    Reviewed 26 September 2017

  
Vancouver, Canada is a beautiful city. We were there in August and the weather was great. 
    Two sights that are a "must see" are Standley Park and the Capilano Suspension Bridge. 
    A drive along the coastline provides great views.

drpanda1
Cape May Court House, New Jersey 

     "Wonderful City"
      Reviewed 14 January 2020

    
Love Love Love Vancouver. I didn't expect to like it this much. 
    We wanted to do something different than go to a resort in the Carribean or Mexico. 
    So despite facing some rainy days, we came to this incredibly beautiful and friendly city.

    The people of Vancouver are so polite and culturally diverse. 
    Tourism is a big industry here so visitors from all around the world do very well here.
     My partner and I were well received here in Vancouver as well as Victoria (on Vancouver Island).

EWanderer
Massachusetts 

     "Takes some time to get to know"
        Reviewed 23 December 2019

      
Unlike many large cities around the world, Vancouver has no must see museum,
       or manmade structure, or tallest anything.
       Rather the whole city is one giant example of Mother 
       Nature and humans coexisting peaceably with one another.
        You won't love Vancouver unless you've stayed there for awhile in one of its many cool,
         quirky neighborhoods and sampled the flavors they have to offer.
         Each neighborhood has its own unique set of stores, yummy restaurants, 
         green parks, and lots of outdoor activities to enjoy.

sandihatteras
Belpre, Ohio 

     "Van Couver a Must See"
          Reviewed 07 March 2021

        
This is a wonderland.....beautiful snow covered mountain tops,
         everything is SO green there.... lovely vibrant colored flowers, forrests,
          huge trees....beautiful mountain streams and waterfalls.The food is excellent...
          a big variety of Resturants...all indiviually different in the most extreme way.
         The city itself is so exciting...people so friendly, shops everywhere...
         farmers markets so to speak....then on to Van Couver Island..
         where you'll take a ferry...with the whale swimming along side. On the Island itself...
         find little shops of art, bakeries....fish markets...etc. The coast is beautiful...
         with grass growing right down to the sand at the beaches...
         Rocky cliffs, wonderful pink sand! This is a must see!!!
